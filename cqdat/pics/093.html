Introduction


In mathematics, a combination refers to a way of selecting some members of a group of things without regard to order. For example, if there were four pieces of fruit on a table: an Apple, an Orange, a Pear, and a Banana, there would be 6 ways to choose 2 fruits: Apple-Orange, Apple-Pear, Apple-Banana, Orange-Pear, Orange-Banana, and Pear-Banana.

In a similar fashion, a fighter pilot can only choose to engage a certain number of enemy targets based on how many weapons their plane has.
